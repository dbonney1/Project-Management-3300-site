What is Project Stakeholder Management?

A project is prosperous when it accomplishes its goals and meets or surpasses the ideas of the stakeholders. Who are the stakeholders? These are the people who are either presently involved in the production of or have an interest in the process and outcome of the work.

In this video Mike Clayton explains what stakeholder management is and how opening up dialog with these stakeholders and engaging them is one of the most important parts of Project Management.

Another great aspect of Project Stakeholder management is learning how to identify who the stakeholders are. In the following video, you are shown how to identify these individuals and how to communicate with them in the best way.

The benefits of conducting good engagement in project stakeholder management is the trust you earn with the stakeholders, the possibility of improving risk management which can save time and money. There are risks with stakeholder management as well. Conflicts and the loss of control of your schedule or agenda can cost not only you but the stakeholders time and money. Balancing effective stakeholder engagement will be a benefit to everyone.
