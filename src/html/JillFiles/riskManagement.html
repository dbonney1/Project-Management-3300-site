Project Risk Management

What is Project Risk Management you ask? Well, it is the method of identifying, analyzing and then reacting to any risk(s) that may arise over the development of a project. This plan will help the project and team remain on their path and meet its goal(s). When do I start using Risk management? Well, it should be a part of the planning process and used to figure out what risk(s) might happen in the project and how to get control of the threat quickly if it does happen. However, looking for risks throughout the entire project is good practice, as you may be able to head off an unforeseen problem. Risk management can entail myriad items on different types of projects. Such as on a large-scale project, strategies could contain vast detailed planning for each risk to ensure relief strategies are in place if issues arise. However, risk management may mean something as a simple prioritized list of risks labeled as high, medium, or low threat(s) to the timetable and project for smaller projects.
