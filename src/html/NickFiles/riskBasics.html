Project Risk Management Basics

What is project risk management?

Project risk management is a technique used by project managers to reduce the risk of potential problems that could harm a project. A risk includes anything or event that could affect workers, processes, technology, or resources involved in the project. One way risk is different from an issue is that risk is not something that a project manager can predict.

What is a positive risk?

The word risk is usually associated with negative things like someone becoming ill and unable to work or someone breaking a component for a project. However, some things are considered positive risks. Risks that could be considered positive could be finishing the project early or getting more customers than you expected.

Response to positive risk

First, a project manager needs to assess the risk and determine if it can exploit and its likelihood of occurring. Next, the risk must be shared. The project might not be able to take full advantage of the risk, and by involving other people, the chance of yielding a positive result increases. Finally, there may be nothing to do whatsoever. This applies to adverse risks as well because sometimes doing nothing is your best option.

Steps in the risk management process

  • Identify the risk
  • Analyze the risk
  • Prioritize the risk
  • Assign an owner to the risk
  • Respond to the risk
  • Monitor the risk

Conclusion

Project risk management is about reducing the chance of risks negatively affecting the course of a project. There are both positive and negative risks, and both can be dealt with in similar ways. Sometimes your best option may be doing nothing at all.
