Project Quality Management Basics

What is project cost management?

Project quality management is about the process and activities associated with figuring out and achieving the quality of a project's deliverables. Quality is a vague word, however. In the case of project quality management, quality is simply what the customer or stakeholder needs or wants from a project. By keeping the focus of project quality management on the customer and stakeholder, the focus can be more narrow and have a better chance of achieving its goals.

Concepts of quality management

Customer Satisfaction

Even if a deliverable meets every requirement outlined by the customer and stakeholders, there is a chance that the finished product is not satisfactory. While creating a deliverable, a project manager should frequently meet with stakeholders to update them on the progress and get feedback.

Continuous Improvement

The idea of continuous improvement is that there will be an ongoing effort through a project to improve aspects of the project. By applying this concept, a project manager will continuously monitor and document any issues that arise.

Conclusion

In conclusion, project quality management is about figuring the activities required to make a good deliverable. Some project quality management concepts are customer satisfaction, where customers and stakeholders provide feedback, and continuous improvement, where an issue is documented and improved on through a project's lifetime.
