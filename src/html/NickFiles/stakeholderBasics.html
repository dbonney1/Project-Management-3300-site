Project Stakeholder Management Basics

What is project stakeholder management?

To make a project a success, a project manager must make sure a project meets all of its objectives and fulfills the projects' expectations. During the duration of a project manager must control stakeholder's expectations and requirements. A project manager should aim to determine each stakeholder's goals and why they were willing to invest.

Stakeholder Management plan

List Stakeholders

The first is to sort stakeholders into two different groups, internal and external stakeholders. Internal stakeholders are typically department heads within the company. External stakeholders are made up of investors, users, media, and other businesses.

Prioritize Stakeholders

Prioritizing stakeholders means determining which external stakeholders have the most significant influence on the project. This also means noting at which stages in the project a specific stakeholder has more influence.

Interview Stakeholders

There are many different questions you can ask a stakeholder during this phase. For example, "why are you interested in this project," "what are your expectations for this project," "How quickly do you see this project rolling out." All of the questions you ask a stakeholder are used to understand whether they feel positive or negative about the project.

Manage Expectations

This is the essential phase of your project stakeholder management plan. During this phase, a project manager will identify which stakeholders will be more involved in each stage. Project managers will create a timeline for when stakeholders need to provide feedback.

Conclusion

To summarize, project stakeholder management is categorizing stakeholders and determining when each stakeholder has more influence in each stage of a project. A project manager's goal is to fulfill all of the expectations of its stakeholders.
