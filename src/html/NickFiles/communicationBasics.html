Project Communication Management Basics

What is project communication management?

Communication is the key to success for any type of project. According to PMI (Project Management Institute) most project failures are due to issues with interpersonal communication. Project communication management is used to ensure communication issue are not a problem. Project communication management consists of three processes that make sure the correct messages are sent, received, and understood by the correct individuals. The three processes include plan communications management, manage communications, and monitor communications.

Plan Communications Management

This first step is to plan how you will manage communication across all stakeholders. This is done by creating a communications management plan. The factors that need to be addressed are, audience, which is a list of all stakeholders including team members, sponsors, and customers. Objective, which is the purpose of your communications. Message, which is the actual content shared. Channel, this is how the message will be delivered.

Manage Communications

After the communications management plan is created it is the project managers role to manage it. This means that a manager will consistently review the plan and update it as needed to reflect the ongoing changes of the project. A manager will collect and analyze data, create messages, distribute communications, storage of communications reports and files, retrieval of stored communications, and the disposal of communications after the closure of the project.

Monitor Communications

This process is about monitoring and controlling communications throughout the lifetime of a project. This includes making sure communications were sent out, confirming communications were received by the proper people, making sure communications were understood, and confirming feedback was provided to the correct project members.

Conclusion

Project communication management ensures proper communication throughout the duration of a project. This is accomplished by the three primary project communication management processes. The three primary project communications management processes are plan communication management, management communications, and monitor communications.
