Project Procurement Management Basics

What is project procurement management?

Project procurement management is about obtaining goods, supplies, and services required to complete a project. These goods, supplies, and services are necessary to make sure a project is successful. Project procurement management is divided into four steps, plan procurement management, conduct procurement, control procurement, and close procurement.

The project procurement management process

Plan procurement management

This step in the project procurement management process will identify what external resources you'll need.

Conduct procurement

This step in the project procurement management process lets you choose the contractors that best fit your needs.

Control procurement

In this step in the project procurement management process, you will manage your hired contractors and keep up with their schedule.

Close procurement

At this stage, you make sure all contracts have been fulfilled and ended properly

Conclusion

Project procurement management is about getting all the goods, supplies, and services required to make a project successful. There are four steps in the project procurement management process: plan procurement management, procurement, control procurement, and close procurement.
